We are beginning to build a DevOps Fluency model for enterprises to identify and articulate the behaviors, benefits, and investment needed to achieve specific outcomes.

At DevOps Enterprise Summit (DOES) 2018, Dr. Nicole Forsgren and Jez Humble, coauthors of *Accelerate: The Science of Lean Software and DevOps*, spoke about how enterprises can become high-performing technology organizations.

One line stood out: maturity models are dumb.

It is a bold statement, but one we strongly agree with for three primary reasons:

1. Maturity models imply an end state, while high-performing organizations never stop improving.
2. Maturity models are often prescriptive and assume one progression path, which rarely matches heterogeneous enterprise realities.
3. Maturity models often emphasize technical proficiency more than value-based outcomes.

## DevOps Fluency Model for Enterprise Transformation

We propose a different approach and invite input from the broader DevOps community.

Instead of a maturity model, enterprises need a mechanism to identify and articulate:

- behaviors
- benefits
- investment required (cultural change, adaptation time, and resources)

These factors should tie directly to desired outcomes.

For this reason, we began building a DevOps Fluency model for enterprise transformation.

## What Is a DevOps Fluency Model?

Drawing inspiration from the Agile Fluency model, DevOps fluency is a non-prescriptive approach that lets organizations choose capabilities based on business context and appetite for change.

In maturity models, success often means reaching the top level.

In a fluency model, teams become fluent when they consistently demonstrate the capabilities of their target level, even under pressure.

An analogy is language learning:

- If I want travel-level Spanish for ordering food and navigating markets, I can target a lighter investment and moderate proficiency.
- If I want to relocate my family indefinitely, I need deeper fluency and a much larger time/effort investment.

The same principle applies to DevOps transformation.

## Collaborate with Us on a DevOps Fluency Model

We do not claim to have all the answers, and we welcome collaboration.

Ways to contribute:

1. Talk with us 1:1 (confidentially) to evaluate whether the model direction is useful.
2. Collaborate with us in building the model.
3. Help us test the model in real environments.
4. Share feedback compared to other approaches.
5. Send general feedback and ideas.
